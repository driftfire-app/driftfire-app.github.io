Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 4, 2026

Driftfire is a local, offline game. We do not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data. This policy explains that in plain terms.

What we collect

Nothing. Driftfire collects no personal data of any kind — no name, email, location, device identifiers, contacts, photos, or usage data.

No tracking, no third parties

Driftfire contains:

  • No analytics and no usage tracking.
  • No advertising and no ad networks.
  • No third-party SDKs that collect data.
  • No accounts or logins.

No network calls

Driftfire does not connect to the internet. The game runs entirely on your device and makes no network requests. Nothing leaves your device because nothing is sent anywhere.

Your data stays on your device

Game progress and settings are saved locally on your device (in the app's private storage). We have no access to these files, and they are never uploaded. Deleting the app removes them.

Children's privacy

Driftfire is safe for players of all ages. Because the game collects no data whatsoever — from anyone, including children — it does not knowingly gather personal information from children under 13 (or the equivalent age in your region). This is consistent with COPPA and similar children's-privacy laws: with no collection, there is nothing to disclose, sell, or retain.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, we will update this page and revise the effective date above.
